Signed stems introduce the notion of truth, as in asserting that a statement is true or false. Verbs can form three stems from a root: neutral {ā}, positive {j}, negative {w}; the former can be referred to as “unsigned”, the latter two as “signed”. Additional variations of this may occur: semipositive {e}, seminegative {o}, etc., through the full range [positive, neutral, negative] {j,ı,ē,ĕ,ā,ŏ,ō,u,w}. Additionally may be (occasionally) used the remaining vowels, viz. {ă,ø,y}. Stems may occur even for which no definitions are given.
