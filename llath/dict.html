Ļaþ Roo English definition/translation Search by Ļaþ Search by English —erb— (P)F(ṼF)(F)[S]
Verbs can form three stems from a root: neutral {ā}, positive {j}, negative {w}; the former can be referred to as “unsigned”, the latter two as “signed”. Additional variations of this may occur: semipositive {e}, seminegative {o}, etc., through the full range [positive, neutral, negative] {j,ı,ē,ĕ,ā,ŏ,ō,u,w}. Additionally may be (occasionally) used the remaining vowels, viz. {ă,ø,y}. Stems may occur even for which no definitions are given.

F = fricative {ṗ,ḃ,f,v,þ,ð,s,z,ś,ź,ç,ķ,ǥ,ḩ,ẇ}
P = plosive {p,b,t,d,k,g}
Ṽ = any vowel {j,ı,ē,ĕ,ā,ŏ,ō,u,w}, nasal {n,ñ,m}, or smooth liquid {l,ṟ}
S = stem vowel {j,ı,ē,ĕ,ā,ŏ,ō,u,w} (typically {ā,j,w})
