Euler's method [ˈoʏ̯lɐ]

Let $f(x)$ be the solution to the differential equation ${dy \over dx}$ which satisfies the initial condition $f(x_0)=y_0$. Euler's method states that $y_{n+1} = y_n + {dy \over dx} \Delta x$ and $x_{n+1} = x_n + \Delta x$, so that $f(x_n) \approx y_n$; that is, $y_n$ is an approximation of $f(x_n)$, the exact solution to the differential equation. As $\Delta x$ gets smaller and smaller, the approximation (generally) becomes more accurate: $\lim_{\Delta x\to 0} y_n = f(x_n)$.
